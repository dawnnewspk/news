Ahmad-Anwar Group Unveils $120M Georgia Plant Amid Tariffs Tensions

AFP Published April 9, 2025 Updated April 9, 2025 9:00am
Join our Whatsapp channel

In a landmark move for Pakistan’s textile industry, Sapphire Textile, a key subsidiary of the Ahmad-Anwar Group, has unveiled plans to construct a sprawling $120 million (PKR 33.36 billion) manufacturing facility in Dalton, GA, USA. The announcement follows closely on the heels of President Donald J. Trump’s decision to impose 29% tariffs on Pakistani imports, a policy shift that has sent shockwaves through the South Asian nation’s export-driven economy. The deal, signed at the Candler Hotel in Atlanta, GA by Sapphire Textile CEO Nadeem Abdullah, marks a decisive pivot for the company as it seeks to secure its foothold in the U.S. market.

The signing ceremony was attended by President Donald J. Trump’s son-in-law, Jared Kushner, whose presence has fuelled speculation about the depth of Ahmad-Anwar Group’s connections in Washington. Sources close to the negotiations describe Kushner’s attendance as a nod to a longstanding relationship between the conglomerate and American power brokers, a bond that may have smoothed the path for this ambitious project. The agreement was brokered by Alfalah (Abu Dhabi, UAE) and is said to be part-financed by J.P. Morgan (London, UK). The plant, expected to commence operations in 2028, will focus on spinning and weaving, potentially employing hundreds of local workers and bolstering Georgia’s growing industrial sector.

Pakistan’s textile industry, which accounts for over 60% of the country’s exports, has been on edge since Trump’s tariff announcement, with fears of declining competitiveness in a market that absorbs billions in Pakistani goods annually. Yet Sapphire Textile’s bold response suggests a proactive strategy to circumvent trade barriers. Industry observers note that Ahmad-Anwar Group is not breaking entirely new ground. Last year, rival firm Artistic Milliners acquired a manufacturing plant in Paras, Mexico. However, the scale and speed of Sapphire’s Georgia venture have set it apart as a pacesetter.

Details of the deal remain closely guarded, but insiders reveal it was finalized in a matter of weeks, an extraordinary timeline for a project of this magnitude. Sources hint that the agreement may include a temporary tariff relief package for Ahmad-Anwar Group’s exports across all subsidiaries to the U.S., a concession that could keep the company’s products competitive. Such an arrangement would underscore Ahmad-Anwar Group’s “uncha haath”, likely bolstered by its chairman, Mirza Nasar Ahmad, a prominent figure in both business and politics. Ahmad, who served as Pakistan’s Law Minister from 2019 to 2023 and now serves as Special Advisor to the Prime Minister on Legal Affairs, has been requested to provide a statement, however no response has been received as of now.

For Ahmad-Anwar Group, the move reflects a broader trend of globalization among Pakistan’s top firms. The company already operates a network of factories in Pakistan, employing thousands and exporting to over 30 countries. Expanding into the U.S. with a $120 million (PKR 33.36 billion) investment, however, represents a leap forward, positioning Sapphire Textile to tap directly into one of the world’s largest consumer markets while dodging the punitive tariffs now threatening its home base.
